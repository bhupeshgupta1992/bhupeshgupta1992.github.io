About the Journal

Indian Journal of Mental and Community Health (IJMCH) is a peer-reviewed, open access monthly journal dedicated to promoting excellence in mental health, community medicine, public health, and interdisciplinary healthcare research.

Aims and Scope

The Indian Journal of Mental and Community Health (IJMCH) is a peer-reviewed, open-access multidisciplinary journal dedicated to advancing knowledge and research in mental health, psychiatry, psychology, public health, and community medicine.

The journal publishes original research articles, systematic reviews, meta-analyses, narrative reviews, case reports, short communications, and editorials.

The scope of the journal includes, but is not limited to:

  • Psychiatry and clinical psychology
  • Mental health promotion and prevention
  • Community psychiatry and psychosocial rehabilitation
  • Public health and community medicine
  • Epidemiology and biostatistics
  • Behavioral sciences and health education
  • Substance use and addiction medicine
  • Child and adolescent mental health
  • Geriatric mental health
  • Women’s mental health
  • Occupational and environmental health
  • Health systems, healthcare management, and policy
  • Primary healthcare and family medicine
  • Digital health, telemedicine, and artificial intelligence in healthcare
  • Non-communicable and communicable diseases
  • Social determinants of health
  • Lifestyle medicine and preventive healthcare
  • Rehabilitation sciences and quality of life research

The journal particularly welcomes interdisciplinary studies that integrate mental health with community-based healthcare approaches and innovative healthcare technologies.

Publication Frequency

Monthly (3 issues per year)

Open Access Policy

The Indian Journal of Mental and Community Health (IJMCH) follows a full open-access publishing model to promote the free exchange of scientific knowledge worldwide.

The journal believes that open access enhances global knowledge exchange, increases the visibility and impact of research, and supports equitable access to scientific literature.

Under this policy:

  • Readers may freely read, download, copy, distribute, print, search, or link to the full texts of published articles without prior permission from the publisher or the author.
  • Authors retain copyright of their published work unless otherwise specified by journal policy.
  • Published content may be shared and used for educational, research, and non-commercial purposes with proper attribution.
  • The journal encourages the dissemination of research findings to researchers, healthcare professionals, policymakers, educators, and the general public.
  • IJMCH is committed to maintaining high ethical standards, rigorous peer review, transparency in publication practices, and academic integrity.
